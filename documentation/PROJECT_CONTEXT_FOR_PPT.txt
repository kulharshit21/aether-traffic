PROBLEM STATEMENT (verbatim)
-------------------------------------
Poor Visibility on Parking-Induced Congestion

-------------------------------------
WHAT WE BUILT (one paragraph, plain English)
-------------------------------------
We built an end-to-end artificial intelligence system that transitions traffic enforcement from reactive ticketing to proactive congestion prevention. By processing nearly 300,000 historical traffic violations, our system automatically detects localized parking hotspots, calculates the actual physical traffic jam (shockwave) caused by the parked cars, and predicts when congestion will peak next. Finally, it acts as a digital dispatcher by generating an optimized schedule that tells police exactly which zones to send limited tow trucks to in order to maximize city-wide traffic flow recovery.

-------------------------------------
THE 4-STAGE PIPELINE
-------------------------------------
Stage 1: Spatial Clustering (HDBSCAN) — We used density-based clustering to map individual violation GPS coordinates into exact physical bottleneck zones without hardcoding city polygons. — Output: 312 exact hotspot zones identified.
Stage 2: Congestion Quantification (Greenshields & LWR Physics) — We applied actual traffic flow physics to translate static parking tickets into dynamic lane capacity loss and upstream queue lengths. — Output: Up to 95.0% capacity loss quantified for the worst bottlenecks.
Stage 3: Predictive Forecasting (XGBoost) — We trained a machine learning model using temporal lags to predict congestion severity 1-3 hours into the future. — Output: XGBoost forecasts for all zones (e.g., Electronic City impact spiking from 261 to 263).
Stage 4: Prescriptive Optimization (0-1 Knapsack) — We treated patrol deployment as a mathematical optimization problem to allocate a limited number of tow trucks to the highest-value zones while accounting for travel time. — Output: A ranked dispatch schedule prioritizing Pulikeshinagar, Bellandur, and K.R. Pura.

-------------------------------------
WHY THIS SOLUTION IS DIFFERENT / WINS
-------------------------------------
- **It is Causal, not Correlative:** Instead of just putting colored dots on a map, we used Lighthill-Whitham-Richards (LWR) shockwave theory to calculate the *actual* lane capacity lost.
- **It is Prescriptive:** It doesn't just show a problem; it provides the mathematical solution. The Knapsack optimization tells the city exactly where to deploy limited tow trucks to maximize ROI.
- **Field-Ready Engineering:** We moved beyond a Jupyter notebook by engineering a production-ready `dispatch_schedule.csv` that directly feeds a daylight-readable, functional command center dashboard.
